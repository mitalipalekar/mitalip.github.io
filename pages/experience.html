LEADERSHIP

UW Society of Women Engineers - Vice President of Community Outreach

For the 2016-17 academic year, as one of the seven executive officers, I organize several events to improve diversity and outreach initiatives for the K-12 community in Seattle, create a comfortable and informal space for women in STEM within our 450+ member organization and collaborate with other organizations both on and off campus to foster a community of collaboration and inclusivity to allow people to fulfil their scientific inflinations. As VP of Community Outreach, I was also responsible for leading my team and outreach committee leads, recruiting volunteers for events such as programming days, workshops, hackathons and maker symposiums as well as collaborating with the Outreach and Diversity efforts through the UW College of Engineering.

UW Girls Who Code - Committe Lead

As the Committee Lead for UW Girls Who Code, I planned majors panels, netoworking events and programming workshops to encourage high school students and UW pre-engineers to explore the fields of computing, technology and computer science. Through this initiative, I wanted to create an environment where young women are comfortable exploring the seemigly male-dominated environments of Computer Science and Engineering while simultaensouly providing them with the resources to succeed academically and professionally.
